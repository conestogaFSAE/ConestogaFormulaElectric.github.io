Formula SAE Electric - Michigan 2026

An all-electric race car, designed and built at Conestoga College.

Conestoga Formula Electric (CFE) is the active program inside Conestoga Racing. Electrical, mechanical, and business teams are building a compliant EV platform for SAE’s 2026 Formula Electric competition at Michigan International Speedway.

Current focus

What we’re working on this season

Accumulator & safety

Cell selection, segment layout, light-weight enclosures, BMS integration, TSAL/IMD logic, and all document packages required by SAE.

Vehicle platform

Suspension geometry, chassis fabrication, steering/braking ergonomics, cooling, aeros, and packaging the powertrain for quick serviceability.

Operations & business

Documentation, cost & design events, campus demos, charitable donation processing, and logistics for taking the team to MIS in 2026.

Team pillars

Three squads, one car

Electrical systems

  • HV accumulator, tractive-system rules, LV harnessing, sensor suites, safety interlocks.
  • Telemetry + data acquisition to accelerate testing and driver feedback loops.

Mechanical systems

  • Chassis concept, hardpoint targets, suspension, steering, braking, cooling, aero.
  • Manufacturing plans that keep the Cambridge/Fountain Street shops efficient.

Business & partnerships

  • Cost report, design presentation, sponsor engagement, donor stewardship.
  • Project management, risk tracking, and documentation for SAE inspections.

Roadmap

Path to Formula SAE Electric 2026

Concept freeze - Fall 2024

Architecture decisions locked for chassis, accumulator, and control systems.

Subsystem prototypes - Winter 2025

Accumulator mock-ups, LV harness testing, pedal box ergonomics, and aero CFD studies.

Manufacturing & integration - Summer/Fall 2025

Tube cutting, weldments, composite layups, dyno runs, and full electrical checkout.

Validation & compliance - Winter 2026

Electrical readiness review, rules documentation, driver-in-loop, and endurance simulations.

Competition - June 2026

Formula SAE Electric at Michigan International Speedway. CFE is already listed on the official attendance/waitlist.

Support

Sponsorships vs. charitable donations

Financial contributions can be processed either as sponsorship (with mutually agreed deliverables) or as charitable donations through Conestoga College. Charitable donations receive a receipt and public acknowledgement, but they are not exchanged for advertising, logo placement, or other quid pro quo benefits.

Sponsorship

Work with our business team on a Bronze/Silver/Gold package aligned to the 2026 build. Typical deliverables include shop visits, technical talks, and design reviews—not paid advertising.

Charitable donation

Make a tax-receipted gift to Conestoga College earmarked for CFE. We recognize donors on our site and reports while keeping the transaction strictly philanthropic.
